Adversarial defense
15 signals. 3 layers.
No GPS spoofer gets through.
A fraud ring of 500 GPS-spoofed accounts just drained a competitor. Clad fights back at every layer.
Layer 1 — Individual
GPS Variance Analysis
Spoofed GPS holds a static coordinate. Real phones show 3–8m natural jitter. Static within ±0.5m for 40+ min = impossible on real hardware.
Layer 1 — Individual
Accelerometer Cross-Check
Fake GPS can't spoof the accelerometer. A fraudster making deliveries shows walking cadence (1.8–2.2 Hz) contradicting their "stationary" GPS.
Layer 1 — Individual
Behavioral Biometrics
30-day fingerprint of tap pressure, scroll velocity, phone tilt. A ring operator using 10 fake accounts on one device triggers all 10.
Layer 1 — Individual
Delivery History Gate + PAN
Real workers have 8+ weeks of history. PAN verification means a ring can't batch-create 500 accounts. No history = no payout eligibility.
Layer 2 — Network
Claim Timestamp Clustering
Real workers file over 90–120 min. Fraud rings script all 500 in 3–5 min. 50+ claims from same pincode in 5 min → batch held.
Layer 2 — Network
Patient Zero Unwinding
Fraud clusters trace back to a referral root. Every account within 2 degrees of Patient Zero enters permanent red lane. Ring unwound retroactively.
Layer 2 — Network
Honeypot Ghost Zones
2–3 ghost pincodes appear valid when probed but have zero real workers. First claim from a ghost zone → entire ring exposed and frozen.
Layer 3 — UX Protection
Tiered Lane Routing — Honest Workers Never Blocked
Claims route to 🟢 Green (auto 2hr), 🟡 Yellow (6hr → auto), or 🔴 Red (24hr review). False positives add a Shield note — never counted against the worker. Network drops in bad weather get 20% signal tolerance. Real disruptions confirmed by 40+ zone neighbours.
